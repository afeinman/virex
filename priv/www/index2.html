Vim Regex Quick Reference (in default/magic mode)

  • .
    Any single character within a line
  • \.
    Literal '.'
  • [abc]
    A single character: a, b, or c
  • [^abc]
    Any character except: a, b, or c
  • ^
    If first, matches start-of-line; otherwise, literal '^'
  • \_^
    Start of line
  • $
    If last, matches start-of-line; otherwise, literal '$'
  • \_$
    End of line
  • \_.
    Any character, including end-of-line
  • \<
    left word boundary
  • \>
    right word boundary
  • \a or [:alpha:]
    alphabetic character
  • [:alnum:]
    Any single letter or digit
  • \d or [:digit:]
    digit
  • \D
    non-digit
  • \l
    lower case character
  • \s or [:space:]
    whitespace character
  • \S
    non-whitespace character
  • \w
    word character
  • \W
    non-word character
  • \u
    upper case character
  • [:punct:]
    Any single punctuation character
  • a\|b
    a or b
  • a\?
    Zero or one of a
  • a*
    Zero or more of a
  • a\+
    One or more of a
  • a\{3}
    Exactly 3 of a
  • a\{3,}
    3 or more of a
  • a\{,6}
    Up to 6 of a
  • a\{3,6}
    Between 3 and 6 of a
  • r\%[ead]
    Longest available of: r, re, rea, read
  • \( ... \)
    Capture everything enclosed into \1, \2, etc.
  • \(.\)a\1
    'yay', 'dad', 'xax', etc.

For detailed documentation on vim's regex, check out vimregex.com, the vim docs, and the vim tips wiki.
